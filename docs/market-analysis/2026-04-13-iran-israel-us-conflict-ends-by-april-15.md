## 2. Scope and Settlement Notes

This market is narrower than headline risk suggests.

- The contract is about `Iran` and `Israel/US` direct qualifying military action.
- `Lebanon` fighting is not automatically enough.
- Proxy activity such as `Hezbollah` or `Houthis` is generally not enough by itself.
- Political tension, failed talks, or Hormuz friction are not automatically enough unless they turn into qualifying direct action under the written rules.

This was the central recurring warning in the prior analysis:

- a lot of scary Middle East headlines are real
- but many of them are not clean settlement evidence for this exact contract

## 5. Why This Contract Is Tricky

The prior analysis consistently came back to the same problem:

1. The broad thesis "the region is still unstable" can be true.
2. The narrower thesis "a qualifying direct Iran-Israel/US action will occur in the relevant rule window" is much harder.
3. A lot of evidence that changes the broad thesis does **not** cleanly change this contract.

In other words:

- direction risk is one thing
- timing risk is another
- written settlement scope is a third thing

This contract forces all three to line up.
